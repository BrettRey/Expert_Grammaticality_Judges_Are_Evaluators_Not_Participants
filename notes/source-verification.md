# Source Verification Notes

Do not add citations or BibTeX entries from memory. Verify source metadata and claims against authoritative sources before citing.

## Source Families To Verify

### Human-Participant / Ethics Guidance

- TCPS 2 definitions of research involving human participants.
- Humber HREB guidance, if this paper explicitly mentions local review practice.
- Journal or publisher policies that distinguish research participants from professional consultation, peer review, expert evaluation, or public/professional role contributions.

### Expert Elicitation And Expert Judgment

- Expert elicitation methodology in risk analysis, medicine, policy, or decision science.
- Standards for documenting expert qualifications, independence, uncertainty, disagreement, and conflict of interest.

### Educational / Psychometric Rater Analogy

- Essay-scoring and constructed-response assessment literature.
- Rater reliability, rater drift, adjudication, moderation, and rubric calibration.
- Generalizability theory or many-facet Rasch models, if the paper needs a measurement-theory bridge.

### Linguistic Judgment Methodology

- Foundational and current work on grammaticality judgments, acceptability judgments, expert intuition, and experimental syntax.
- Sources distinguishing grammaticality, acceptability, processing difficulty, register, dialect, and social distribution.

### Research-Role Boundary Cases

- Cases where expert judges are themselves the research target: expert/novice contrasts, linguist/non-linguist judgment comparisons, demographic or dialectal judgment distributions, response-time studies, or training/intervention studies.

## Candidate Claim Checks

- Whether ethics frameworks explicitly exclude or differently handle expert consultation.
- Whether educational raters are normally treated as measurement apparatus, study personnel, contractors, or research subjects in psychometric studies.
- Whether linguistic judgment papers have already made the participant/evaluator distinction under another label.
- Whether REB/HREB terminology requires `participant` even for cases that are methodologically evaluator-like.
