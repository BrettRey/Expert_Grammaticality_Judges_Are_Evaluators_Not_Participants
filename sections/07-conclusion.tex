\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Expert grammaticality judgments should be classified by evidential role. When the research target is a population of judgments, an effect on judges, or the social distribution of acceptability, judges are participants. When the target is the linguistic status of materials and expert judgment is used to evaluate those materials, judges are evaluators.

The payoff is methodological hygiene. It lets grammaticality research be transparent about expert judgment without pretending that every expert consultation is a human-participant study. The right analogy is often not the survey respondent but the essay scorer: a fallible, reportable, calibratable evaluator whose work affects measurement quality.
