# DECISIONS

2026-06-18 — Created the project as a standalone paper under `papers/Expert_Grammaticality_Judges_Are_Evaluators_Not_Participants/`. Reason: the argument is a general methodological and ethics-facing claim about the evidential role of expert grammaticality judges, not a subargument of one grammaticality paper.

2026-06-18 — Working title set to *Expert Grammaticality Judges Are Evaluators, Not Participants: Grammaticality Judgments, Rater Roles, and Human-Subjects Review*. Reason: the title foregrounds the participant/evaluator distinction while leaving room for ethics-review and measurement-quality implications.

2026-06-18 — Central framing fixed as role-based, not status-based. Reason: expert judges are participants when the study targets their responses, processing, background, or distribution; they are evaluators when their expertise is used to assess linguistic materials.

2026-06-18 — Added the essay-evaluator / psychometric-rater analogy as the guiding comparison. Reason: scorer reliability, bias, drift, and adjudication are real methodological issues, but they do not make scorers participants in the same sense as the people whose performances are being measured.

2026-06-18 — Set project to central bibliography and central house style by symlink. Reason: current paper infrastructure avoids local house-style snapshots and uses `references-local.bib` only for verified project-specific entries.
